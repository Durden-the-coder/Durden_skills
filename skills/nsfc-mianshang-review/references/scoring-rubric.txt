NSFC GENERAL PROGRAM REVIEW RUBRIC

Use this reference only when consistent rating standards are needed.

SCIENTIFIC SIGNIFICANCE
High:
- The central scientific question is clear, important, and basic-research oriented.
- The proposal addresses a genuine unresolved mechanism, principle, or conceptual gap.
- The field context is accurate and current.

Medium:
- The topic is relevant, but the scientific question is broad, partly derivative, or not sharply framed.
- The research gap exists but needs clearer positioning.

Low:
- The proposal is mainly technical, descriptive, or application-driven.
- The scientific question is vague, already answered, or not connected to the proposed work.

INNOVATION
High:
- The proposal introduces a genuinely new concept, mechanism, model, method, or research angle.
- The innovation claim is supported by literature gap and preliminary evidence.

Medium:
- The work has incremental novelty, but the main framework resembles existing studies.
- Novelty depends heavily on context, cohort, model system, or technical combination.

Low:
- The proposal repackages known methods or routine extensions.
- Novelty claims are overstated or not linked to a scientific advance.

RESEARCH CONTENT AND APPROACH
High:
- Aims are coherent and mutually supportive.
- Methods directly test the core hypothesis.
- Controls, models, statistics, validation, and endpoints are appropriate.

Medium:
- The overall design is plausible, but key links are underdeveloped.
- Some controls, validation steps, or statistical details need strengthening.

Low:
- Aims are disconnected or mostly technical.
- The technical route cannot answer the scientific question.
- Critical controls, validation, or feasibility steps are missing.

RESEARCH BASIS AND FEASIBILITY
High:
- Preliminary data and prior work strongly support the proposed direction.
- Team expertise and conditions match the required methods.
- Risks and alternatives are concrete and credible.

Medium:
- The team has relevant basis, but key preliminary support or risk handling is incomplete.

Low:
- Feasibility relies on unsupported assumptions.
- Key resources, expertise, preliminary data, or alternatives are missing.

REVIEW IMPACT
Major concern:
- A flaw that may affect funding priority, scientific validity, feasibility, or innovation.

Minor concern:
- A fixable issue that improves clarity, completeness, or presentation but does not change the main judgment.
