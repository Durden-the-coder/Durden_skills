NSFC GENERAL PROGRAM SCORING RUBRIC

Use this file to keep review judgments consistent across proposals. It is not an automatic scoring machine. Final grades must combine weighted scoring, cap rules, ethics/compliance gates, and strict internal triage.

GENERAL 0-5 SCALE

5: Excellent. Strong evidence, clear logic, and no substantive weakness in this dimension.
4: Good. Mostly convincing, with minor issues that do not materially affect competitiveness.
3: Moderate. The dimension is present but needs clear strengthening.
2: Weak. Problems materially lower funding priority.
1: Very weak. The dimension barely supports the proposal.
0: Missing, incorrect, contradicted by evidence, or irrelevant to the proposal's core claim.

ETHICS AND COMPLIANCE GATE

Do not include ethics/compliance in the 100-point weighted score. Treat it as a gate:

PASS: No obvious ethics, compliance, confidentiality, biosafety, AI-disclosure, or sensitive-information issue.
CONCERN: Requires human checking but does not automatically block review.
BLOCKER: Serious ethics/compliance/confidentiality risk. Stop normal scoring and request human clarification.

RESEARCH ATTRIBUTE CHECK

Record the applicant-selected attribute:
- free_exploration
- goal_oriented
- unclear

Then judge whether the proposal content matches the selected attribute.

FREE EXPLORATION CHAIN

Academic frontier or unknown phenomenon
-> basic scientific question
-> original hypothesis/mechanism/principle
-> generalizable new knowledge

GOAL-ORIENTED BASIC RESEARCH CHAIN

Economic/social/national/clinical need
-> key bottleneck
-> underlying unknown basic scientific problem
-> mechanistic research in this proposal
-> generalizable new knowledge

ATTRIBUTE MISMATCH LEVELS

None: selected attribute and proposal logic are aligned.
Mild: wording is imperfect, but the scientific logic still supports the selected attribute.
Moderate: label and main logic are visibly misaligned, but a scientific question still exists. Maximum grade: B.
Severe: the selected attribute is central to the proposal's logic but the attribute chain fails. Treat as a decision-level defect; usually grade C.

FREE EXPLORATION RUBRIC

Weights:
- Scientific question and originality: 30
- Literature novelty and field positioning: 20
- Mechanistic hypothesis and logical closure: 20
- Technical route and key experimental design: 15
- Preliminary basis, feasibility, and risk control: 15

1. Scientific question and originality (30)
5: Highly focused basic scientific question; clear unknown mechanism/principle/concept; strong originality and frontier value.
4: Clear scientific question with meaningful originality, but the boundary can be sharpened.
3: Valid question, but somewhat conventional or incremental; may rely on a new object/model/context.
2: Vague scientific question; reads like a task list; weak originality.
1: No clear scientific question; mostly "study gene/pathway/model X".
0: Question is invalid or already clearly answered.

2. Literature novelty and field positioning (20)
5: Key literature is accurately covered; the unresolved gap is well demonstrated.
4: Literature coverage is solid; novelty mostly stands, with limited missing context.
3: Some novelty exists, but close prior work requires clearer differentiation.
2: Field positioning is weak; obvious related work is missing; novelty boundary is unclear.
1: Core innovation is largely covered by prior literature.
0: Literature directly undermines the novelty claim.

3. Mechanistic hypothesis and logical closure (20)
5: Central hypothesis is clear, causal chain is complete, and key experiments can close the mechanism.
4: Mechanistic chain is mostly convincing, with a few links needing strengthening.
3: Hypothesis is plausible but broad; causal links contain jumps.
2: Multiple mechanism directions are listed without a main line; decisive causal tests are missing.
1: Mechanism is post hoc or descriptive; no falsifiable hypothesis.
0: Mechanistic hypothesis conflicts with evidence or cannot be tested by the proposal.

4. Technical route and key experimental design (15)
5: Methods directly answer the scientific question; models, controls, statistics, validation, and endpoints are strong.
4: Design is generally appropriate; minor details need improvement.
3: Feasible but conventional; key validation is not decisive.
2: Experimental pile-up; some methods do not answer the core question.
1: Key experiments are missing or disconnected from the question.
0: Technical route cannot test the central hypothesis.

5. Preliminary basis, feasibility, and risk control (15)
5: Strong preliminary data; team/platform/methods fit the project; risks and alternatives are concrete.
4: Good basis; major risks appear controllable.
3: Some basis exists, but key preliminary evidence or alternatives are incomplete.
2: Weak basis; workload or risk is high; alternatives are generic.
1: Depends on unverified assumptions or missing capability.
0: Lacks key conditions or capability needed to execute.

GOAL-ORIENTED BASIC RESEARCH RUBRIC

Weights:
- Need-bottleneck-basic-science match: 25
- Mechanistic chain and bottleneck explanatory power: 25
- Basic-research nature and translational boundary: 15
- Difference from literature/existing solutions and innovation: 15
- Technical route and key validation design: 10
- Preliminary basis, feasibility, and risk control: 10

1. Need-bottleneck-basic-science match (25)
5: Need is specific and important; bottleneck is clear; underlying basic scientific question is explicit and direct.
4: Need and bottleneck are mostly clear; scientific question matches well.
3: Need is valid, but connection to the basic scientific question needs strengthening.
2: Need is slogan-like; bottleneck or scientific question is vague.
1: Need appears attached after the fact; weak relation to the research question.
0: No real goal-oriented chain or not goal-oriented basic research.

2. Mechanistic chain and bottleneck explanatory power (25)
5: Mechanistic research directly explains the target bottleneck; causal chain is closable and explanatory.
4: Mechanistic chain is mostly convincing; a few key nodes need strengthening.
3: Hypothesis is plausible, but explanatory power for the bottleneck is limited.
2: Visible jump between mechanism and target bottleneck.
1: Mechanism work is scattered and does not answer the bottleneck.
0: Mechanistic chain fails or the project is mainly phenotype/efficacy observation.

3. Basic-research nature and translational boundary (15)
5: Clearly basic research; application context is a driver, but core output is mechanism/principle/law.
4: Basic-research nature is clear, with some translational extension.
3: Basic research and application validation are mixed; boundary needs tightening.
2: Large share is drug efficacy, product, protocol, process, or scheme validation.
1: Mostly applied development with weak basic scientific content.
0: Not basic research.

4. Difference from literature/existing solutions and innovation (15)
5: Clearly identifies why existing solutions do not answer the basic bottleneck; innovation is strong.
4: Difference is mostly clear; some recent literature or solution comparison should be added.
3: Some difference exists, but innovation is incremental.
2: Similar mechanisms/solutions exist; difference is unclear.
1: Main idea is largely covered by existing work.
0: Literature or existing solutions directly negate the innovation claim.

5. Technical route and key validation design (10)
5: Design directly validates the need-bottleneck-mechanism chain; key controls are strong.
4: Route is reasonable, with some key validation to strengthen.
3: Feasible but not decisive for the bottleneck.
2: Methods are scattered; validation chain does not close.
1: Key validation is missing.
0: Route cannot answer the goal-oriented scientific question.

6. Preliminary basis, feasibility, and risk control (10)
5: Preliminary data, samples/models, team capability, and risk plans are strong.
4: Good basis; risk is mostly controllable.
3: Some basis exists, but key translational condition or preliminary evidence is insufficient.
2: Weak basis; execution risk is high.
1: Key conditions are absent.
0: Cannot support implementation.

WEIGHTED SCORE

For each dimension:
weighted_points = raw_score / 5 * weight

Total score is the sum of weighted_points. Use this total only as one input to final grade.

CAP RULES

Core scientific question unclear: maximum B.
Novelty substantially covered by literature: maximum B; severe cases C.
Technical route cannot answer the core question: maximum C.
Preliminary basis cannot support key experiments: maximum B or C depending on severity.
Goal-oriented chain broken: maximum B; severe cases C.
Applied development rather than basic research: maximum C.
Ethics/compliance BLOCKER: stop normal scoring and request human handling.
Two or more structural flaws: default C.
One critical decision-level defect: usually C.
Moderate attribute mismatch: maximum B.
Severe attribute mismatch: usually C.

ABC GRADE MAPPING

A: Weighted score >= 80, no decision-level defect, no severe cap rule, and strict internal triage does not trigger C.
B: Weighted score 65-79, or meaningful but fixable concerns, or cap rules limit the proposal to B.
C: Weighted score < 65, or any cap rule triggers C, or strict internal triage finds two or more structural flaws or one critical decision-level defect.

Do not use plus/minus grades unless the user explicitly asks for within-grade ranking. For standard NSFC-style review, output A/B/C only.
