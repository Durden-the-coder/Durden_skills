NSFC Review Ranking Rubric

Purpose:
This rubric is for cross-project ranking and calibration. It does not replace the
per-project free-exploration or goal-oriented rubric used by nsfc-mianshang-review.

Score fields:
- source_score: score extracted from 05_final_review.txt, usually "Total weighted score: x/100".
- calibrated_ranking_score: cross-batch score after comparing all proposals.

Total calibrated score: 100.

1. Scientific question and significance: 20
- 18-20: clear basic-science question, strong significance, not merely technical extension.
- 14-17: important topic, but question framing has some looseness.
- 10-13: significance exists, but scientific question is generic or underdeveloped.
- 0-9: weak question, mostly descriptive or application-driven without clear basic mechanism.

2. Novelty and literature position: 25
- 22-25: convincing novelty with current literature boundary.
- 17-21: some novelty, but not fully decisive.
- 11-16: novelty is incremental or partially occupied.
- 0-10: novelty claim largely unsupported or already known.

3. Mechanistic logic and aim coherence: 20
- 18-20: aims close the central mechanism chain.
- 14-17: mostly coherent, with one important gap.
- 8-13: aims are related but diffuse or do not decisively test the hypothesis.
- 0-7: logic chain is broken or mostly a technique list.

4. Design rigor and feasibility: 20
- 18-20: decisive experiments, proper controls, realistic endpoints.
- 14-17: feasible but some design details are weak.
- 8-13: major feasibility or rigor concerns.
- 0-7: design cannot answer the question or is unrealistic.

5. Preliminary basis and team fit: 10
- 9-10: direct preliminary evidence and credible team fit.
- 7-8: generally adequate.
- 4-6: indirect or thin support.
- 0-3: central claims lack preliminary support or team fit is doubtful.

6. Writing focus and reviewability: 5
- 5: clear, concise, easy to evaluate.
- 3-4: generally readable with some ambiguity.
- 1-2: diffuse, vague, hard to score.
- 0: unreadable or internally inconsistent.

Calibration guidance:
- Use source_score as an anchor when it exists and is consistent with the final review text.
- Adjust only after comparing all proposals in the batch.
- Explain any calibrated_ranking_score shift of 5 points or more from source_score.
- Use 1-3 point gaps for near ties.
- Use 5-10 point gaps for clear qualitative differences.
- Use 10+ point gaps for different fundability classes.
- Avoid identical calibrated scores unless two proposals are genuinely indistinguishable.

Penalty anchors:
- subtract 8-15 for weak or already-occupied novelty claims.
- subtract 8-15 for unclosed mechanism chains.
- subtract 5-12 for overloaded aims or diffuse task structure.
- subtract 5-10 for insufficient preliminary support for central high-risk claims.
- subtract 3-8 for unclear feasibility, team fit, or manual-review uncertainty.
- subtract 3-6 for poor writing, vague endpoints, or hard-to-review structure.

Grade mapping:
A+: 90-100, outstanding or clear priority for support.
A: 85-89, recommended for support.
A-: 80-84, fundable and can be considered for support.
B+: 75-79, borderline but relatively strong.
B: 70-74, borderline.
B-: 65-69, borderline but relatively weak.
C+: 55-64, not recommended, but with local strengths or salvageable elements.
C: 45-54, not recommended.
C-: 0-44, clearly not recommended; structural defects dominate.

Use only A/B/C grades with +/- in final reports. Keep the 100-point score for ordering and calibration.

Input provenance audit:
A: 01-06 files are complete and major stage provenance is explicit.
A-: 01-06 files are complete and fallback/nonstandard provenance is documented consistently.
B: final review is substantive, but some stage files are missing, legacy, or have unknown provenance.
C: final review is missing/thin, protocol-violating, or scoring depends heavily on partial stage files.
D: required files are placeholders, missing, unreadable, or clearly incomplete.

Input quality is a confidence label. It should not automatically lower the scientific score, but it should be reported and used to avoid overconfident fine ranking.
